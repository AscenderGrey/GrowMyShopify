# Anatomy of a High-Converting Landing Page

This document outlines the ideal structure for a high-converting landing page, based on proven best practices. The layout is vertical and linear, guiding visitors from awareness to action. Key principles: Keep it simple, benefit-focused, and objection-handling. Use high-contrast CTAs, minimal distractions, and mobile optimization.

## 1. Header
- **Elements**:
  - Your company logo (top-left).
  - Navigation links (e.g., Link Link Link Link) – keep to main ones only.
  - Primary CTA button (e.g., "CTA") – make it sticky if possible.
- **Tips**:
  - Only main links and sticky CTA.
  - Avoid clutter; focus on essential navigation.

## 2. Heading Section
- **Elements**:
  - Main Heading (bold, large font).
  - Subheading (explanatory text).
  - Call-To-Action (CTA) button (e.g., "Call-To-Action (CTA)").
- **Tips**:
  - What is your USP? What are you selling?
  - Use benefit-driven language – no vague, clever words.
  - Explain the heading's claim in detail with product features.

## 3. Explainer Video
- **Elements**:
  - Embedded video player (e.g., 2-3 minute max video explaining product).
- **Tips**:
  - Use 2-3 minutes max explaining how your product works.
  - What's the next step you want visitors to take?

## 4. Social Proof
- **Elements**:
  - Big company logos for social proof (e.g., in a row or carousel).
- **Tips**:
  - Display logos of well-known companies or partners to build credibility.

## 5. Benefits
- **Elements**:
  - List or grid of benefits (e.g., icons or boxes with short descriptions).
- **Tips**:
  - Features tell, but benefits sell.
  - Focus on how the product improves the user's life, not just what it does.

## 6. How It Works
- **Elements**:
  - Step-by-step breakdown (e.g., numbered or bulleted list with icons).
- **Tips**:
  - Break down your product's functionality into 3-5 easy steps.

## 7. Testimonials
- **Elements**:
  - User testimonials (e.g., quotes, photos, or cards).
- **Tips**:
  - Case studies, prestigious company logos, verified badges, testimonials.
  - Use real, verifiable proof to address skepticism.

## 8. FAQ
- **Elements**:
  - Accordion or list of questions and answers.
- **Tips**:
  - Simple answers to common questions.
  - Handle objections like pricing, setup, or compatibility.

## 9. Final CTA
- **Elements**:
  - Prominent Call-To-Action button (e.g., "Call-To-Action (CTA)").
- **Tips**:
  - High contrast, action-oriented CTA.
  - Place it prominently before the footer to seal the conversion.

## 10. Footer
- **Elements**:
  - Company logo.
  - Links (e.g., Link Link Link Link).
  - Copyright, privacy policy, contact info.
  - Social media icons.
  - Email sign-up form (optional).
- **Tips**:
  - Footer includes: logo, copyright, privacy policy, contact info, social media icons, email sign-up etc.
  - Keep it clean and functional.

## Additional Notes
- **Overall Design Principles**:
  - Vertical scroll layout for easy reading.
  - Use visuals (images, icons) to break up text.
  - A/B test elements like headlines and CTAs.
  - Source: withsupabase.com (as noted in the diagram).
- **Conversion Optimization Tips**:
  - Reduce load times and distractions.
  - Ensure CTAs are above the fold where possible.
  - Track metrics like bounce rate and conversion rate post-implementation.

This structure is adaptable; prioritize based on your audience and product.
